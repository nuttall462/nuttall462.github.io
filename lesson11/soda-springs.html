It's Soda, not Pop

by: Rylee Nuttall

This summer was one of the best to go hiking. Soda Springs saw a record number of hikers on its public trails, beating last year's record by a 200 margin. The mayor takes note of this and considers it a success as part of his "Living Healthier Idaho" initiative. He appreciates everyone's participationg, whether intentional or not. As we encourage others' to live healthier around us, eventually the whole town will be in on the fun. As winter has come, there is one last community hike scheduled for December 5th. Details are on the town calendar.

On a unhealthy note, a debate has sparked recently in the town. Who doesn't love soda?! Or should we say "pop?" The term "pop" instead of soda is widely used in the midwestern part of the United States. According to a 2013 study, the state of Idaho is one of the states that widely uses "soda" instead. Why is that? The actual name for a carbonated beverage is a soda pop. Over the years, it has been abbreviated to soda or pop. The question is, why would someone abbreviate it based on the second word and not the first? Most English abbreviations are taken from the first one or two syllables, so it is quite odd that "pop" got popular. I suppose the name of our town is the correct answer to this debate.
